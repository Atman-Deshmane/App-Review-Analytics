## Weekly App Review Pulse: Instagram Leadership Briefing
**Reporting Period:** 15 Feb 2026 to 17 Feb 2026

### Executive Summary
This reporting period highlights a significant surge in user dissatisfaction, predominantly driven by persistent app bugs and glitches, as evidenced by a substantial impact score. A deeply concerning trend also emerges around content moderation, with users reporting inadequate responses to hate speech, overshadowing other minor issues.

### 1. Top Themes & Strategic Insights

*   **App Bugs & Glitches (Impact Score: 1270, Review Count: 43):** This theme continues to be a major pain point for users. The dominant complaint revolves around unexpected app behavior, such as automatic scrolling, which users perceive as a regression rather than an improvement. This indicates a disconnect between recent feature rollouts and user experience expectations.
*   **Other (Impact Score: 3569, Review Count: 116):** While broad, the "Other" category's high impact score is largely fueled by a significant number of reviews concerning content moderation and perceived inaction against hate speech. This suggests a critical breakdown in trust regarding safety and community standards.
*   **Content & Feed Problems (Impact Score: 280, Review Count: 13):** Users are expressing frustration with the explore feed's increasing reliance on Reels, impacting their ability to browse casually and access content with dated context. This points to a desire for more control and diverse content presentation in exploration areas.

### 3. Recommended Actions

*   **Immediate review and reinforcement of content moderation policies and training, particularly concerning hate speech and discriminatory content, to restore user trust.** Prioritize prompt and decisive action on reported violations that clearly fall outside community guidelines.
*   **Investigate and address the "auto-scrolling" bug urgently, as it is causing significant user frustration and perception of a degraded experience.** Gather more granular data on its occurrence and the affected user segments.
*   **Re-evaluate the algorithm and presentation of content within the Explore feed, offering users greater control and a more balanced mix of content formats beyond just Reels.** Consider options for users to customize their discovery experience.
